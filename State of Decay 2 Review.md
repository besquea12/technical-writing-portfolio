State of Decay 2: Juggernaut Edition Review

Welcome to State of Decay 2, the second installment of the successful
zombie survival game developed by Undead Labs and published by Microsoft
studios released in March 2020. After the success of the original State
of Decay released in June 2013, Undead Labs once again exceeded
expectations with this sequel. The State of Decay series revolutionizes
the zombie genre with its third-person open-world survival game that
focuses on not only killing the infected but on locating enough
resources to endure the apocalypse. As a fan of horror games like
Resident Evil and Silent Hill, State of Decay 2 establishes itself as a
challenging horror simulation where gamers must learn to navigate a
complex world.

Players brave enough to begin their journey through State of Decay
learns to establish bases in different areas of the map. Players receive a small base where the main objective is to build workshops,
storage, beds, and infirmaries for members of the community to utilize.
This is vital to State of Decay's gameplay since players encounter
life-threatening tasks in their journey and must use these essential
facilities to remain alive or the character dies, thus becoming
unplayable for the remainder of the campaign. The other survivors in
one's community are playable, so this provides gamers with the ability
to switch between various characters with diverse skills that are useful
and important for succeeding. I admit it may feel overwhelming to
learn the basics as beginners to this sandbox universe so players must
be attentive during the tutorial.

Players learn to craft items like ammo, arrows, Molotov's, pipe
bombs, along with medical supplies such as first aid kits, painkillers,
and plague cures that are vital if bitten by a zombie. Surviving is key
as I mentioned earlier, scavenging through different homes and former
establishments for supplies is a necessary task to maintain high morale
within one's base. Failure to collect enough resources results in
other characters starting fights, complaining, or abandoning your
community due to a lack of provisions. Aiding survivors, not in your
community will reward players with unique merchandise or weapons,
allowing players the option to explore the world of State Decay which is
filled with unique items.

State of Decay 2 relies on the Unreal Engine 5 to power this beautiful
world of zombies and appealing sites. Traveling through the vast
locations will immediately catch the player\'s attention, however, the
varied zombies one will encounter will steal the show. Undead Labs'
distinctive model for the infected is special, some have red eyes to
signify their level of infection, while others like the Ferro with its
speed, and the juggernaut is a giant beast that can tear characters limb
from limb with ease. State of Decay doesn't shy away from the gore when
killing zombies and I'm glad. Using a crossbow to split open a zombie's
head feels so satisfying but using shotguns, pistols, or rifles to
inflict collateral damage to multiple zombies is a feeling that never
gets old.

One of the coolest aspects in State of Decay is the day and night
feature that makes this simulation top tier. Sure, plenty of zombies are
wandering the streets or stuck inside areas not yet explored in the day
but once the sun goes down, the horror factor commences. The game is
more unforgiving at night due to hordes of zombies invading the streets
and highways, eliminating these hordes can be difficult especially if
other hordes are close by to hear the commotion. Choosing to be
stealthier is always recommended as one can avoid zombies attacking
while looting. Running or using weapons without silencers will draw
unwanted attention but also jeopardize a mission so it's crucial to
remain careful and observant. The nightmare doesn't end despite being
behind the safe walls of one's base, the game keeps players on their
toes with random zombie attacks at the community requiring players to
defend it or risk losing other members and resources.

State of Decay is filled with endless paths to choose from, while some
players may find the game irresistible, I'm aware others may feel
discouraged because of the responsibilities players must attend to for
survival. Changing the difficulty settings does help, however, I find
the zombies become less of a threat and more passive during gameplay
which ultimately impacts this heart-racing experience. Despite Unreal
Engines' impressive tools in creating the world of State of Decay, some
of its shortcomings are present in the driving mechanics and learning
skills to build facilities. The game is filled with abandoned vehicles,
overgrown bushes, and trees so attempting to drive through the
environment is difficult.

One's vehicle does take damage if zombies attack or climb the hood,
requiring players to find or manufacture toolboxes to fix their engines
or risk it becoming immobile. I recommend sticking to the main roads for
easy access in unfamiliar regions to save gasoline in the process. Yes,
you must put gas in the vehicles, another resource that may be difficult
to obtain early in the game. Building facilities in one's base is also a
challenge players must overcome, locating textbooks that teach
characters different skill sets such as gardening, construction,
cooking, or chemistry are essential resources required to upgrade or
improve one's base and influence. Locating the various books isn't easy
but it may also limit the player\'s progression making the game feel
uneventful and static.

State of Decay's attention to detail is astounding, if humans were
living in a simulation this game would be it. Despite continuous tedious
tasks to obtain supplies, the campaign provides players with a deep
narrative of survivors with unique stories to tell. Most missions
include helping others locate personal items and some missions involve
helping others clear infestations of 10 or more zombies. For the most
part, the plague hearts located through the map will be the greatest
challenge of them all, requiring plenty of explosives and ammo to
complete. These plague hearts are usually found inside small rooms or
garages, surrounded by red-eyed zombies that are relentless yet
overpowering to kill and escape from.

Clearing plague hearts is also one of the most rewarding tasks State of
Decay offers exclusive weapons or supplies for the community so be sure
to clear them carefully. Co-op multiplayer is a welcomed addition the
first installment lacked. Players can join their friend's adventure and
help them accomplish a similar task. The downside of this feature is
most of the materials you find will be donated to your friend's base.
However, players can keep any weapons or clothing while also gaining
experience points along with multiplayer rewards for helping friends.

State of Decay 2: Juggernaut Edition will have players coming back for
more action, I've had to remove myself from the game after being
consumed for several hours of gameplay. It becomes addicting once
players learn to survive in this open-world gem, allowing them to secure
larger bases, each providing advantageous assets to utilize. State of
Decay 2 requires patience so attempting to rush through the game will be
difficult, especially alone. Undead Labs continue to support this game
with updates that improve performance, graphics, audio, and gameplay
mechanics. Their latest update introduced five additional maps for
players to enjoy, each map is filled with contrasting environments and
new challenges that require players to adjust their approach.

State of Decay 2 has revolutionized the horror genre with its open-world
sandbox simulation. Despite competing with popular horror titles in the
gaming industry, Undead Labs continues to display its talent and
willingness to create one-of-a-kind experiences with State of Decay. I
encourage gamers to dive into this world of zombies and test their
survival skills, good luck.
